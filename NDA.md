# Non-Disclosure Agreement

**Effective Date:** The date on which the Recipient (as defined below) commits their signed copy of this Agreement to this repository.

**Disclosing Party:** David Cahill, an individual ("Disclosing Party"), acting on his own behalf and on behalf of each of the following entities and ventures, now existing or later formed, that David Cahill owns, controls, or operates as a sole principal (collectively, the "Covered Entities"):

- David Cahill (individually);
- The **Furbidden** GitHub organization and any affiliated project, product, service, or brand operating under that name;
- **Cybrix.AI** and any affiliated project, product, service, or brand;
- Any other sole business venture, initiative, project, brand, or entity formed, acquired, or operated by David Cahill at any time after the Effective Date.

**Recipient:** The individual identified in the signature file committed to the `signatures/` directory of this repository, who, by committing such file, agrees to be bound by this Agreement.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Disclosing Party wishes to share, and the Recipient wishes to receive, certain Confidential Information (as defined below) in connection with exploring, contributing to, collaborating on, being employed by, advising, investing in, or otherwise participating in the business, technical, creative, and strategic activities of one or more of the Covered Entities (the "Purpose"). The parties enter into this Agreement to protect such Confidential Information.

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"**Confidential Information**" means any and all non-public information, in any form or medium, whether tangible or intangible, that is disclosed, made available, observed, overheard, or otherwise received by the Recipient from or about any Covered Entity or the Disclosing Party, whether before, on, or after the Effective Date, including without limitation:

(a) source code, object code, algorithms, models, weights, training data, prompts, datasets, schemas, architectures, infrastructure, APIs, and technical documentation;

(b) business ideas, concepts, plans, strategies, roadmaps, product designs, user experience designs, marketing plans, pricing, financial information, projections, investor materials, and customer or user information;

(c) inventions, discoveries, know-how, trade secrets, processes, methods, formulas, research, experiments, and works in progress, whether patentable or not;

(d) any conversations, messages, emails, chats, calls, voice notes, video recordings, meeting notes, whiteboards, drafts, screenshots, and communications of any kind — whether conducted in person, by phone, by video, by text, by email, through any messaging platform, through any collaboration tool, through social media, through any AI assistant, or through any other method now known or hereafter invented — that relate in any way to any Covered Entity or to the Disclosing Party's business ideas, ventures, or activities;

(e) the identities of, and any information about, other collaborators, contractors, employees, advisors, investors, customers, partners, or counterparties of any Covered Entity;

(f) the existence, terms, status, or substance of any negotiation, relationship, agreement, or dispute involving any Covered Entity;

(g) any information marked or identified as confidential, proprietary, or similar, and any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given its nature or the circumstances of disclosure; and

(h) any notes, analyses, summaries, derivatives, or other materials prepared by the Recipient that contain, reflect, or are based on any of the foregoing.

Confidential Information is confidential regardless of whether it is labeled "confidential" and regardless of the medium or method of disclosure.

## 3. Exclusions

Confidential Information does **not** include information that the Recipient can demonstrate, through contemporaneous written records:

(a) was lawfully in the Recipient's possession, without obligation of confidentiality, prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

(b) is or becomes publicly available through no act or omission of the Recipient or any person bound by an obligation of confidentiality;

(c) was lawfully disclosed to the Recipient by a third party who had the right to make such disclosure without obligation of confidentiality; or

(d) is independently developed by the Recipient without any use of or reference to the Confidential Information.

## 4. Obligations of the Recipient

The Recipient shall:

(a) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and protect it with at least the same degree of care the Recipient uses to protect their own most sensitive information, and in no event less than a reasonable degree of care;

(b) use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose whatsoever, including without limitation any personal, commercial, competitive, or third-party purpose;

(c) not disclose, publish, transmit, post, share, reproduce, or make available any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party (email from David Cahill, or a merged pull request in a Covered Entity repository authored by David Cahill, constitutes written consent);

(d) not use Confidential Information to train, fine-tune, evaluate, prompt, or otherwise feed any artificial intelligence or machine learning system that is not controlled by a Covered Entity, and not upload or submit Confidential Information to any third-party service that retains, learns from, or may disclose such information, without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(e) not reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or attempt to derive the underlying ideas, structure, or algorithms of any Confidential Information;

(f) not file any patent, trademark, copyright, or other intellectual property application that incorporates, is derived from, or is based on any Confidential Information;

(g) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure, access, loss, or use of Confidential Information;

(h) take reasonable measures to prevent such unauthorized disclosure or use, including by securing any device, account, or storage medium on which Confidential Information is stored.

## 5. Compelled Disclosure

If the Recipient is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information by subpoena, court order, or other legal process, the Recipient shall, to the extent legally permitted, (i) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing, (ii) cooperate with the Disclosing Party's reasonable efforts to seek a protective order or other remedy, and (iii) disclose only the minimum Confidential Information legally required.

## 6. No License; Ownership

Nothing in this Agreement grants the Recipient any license, right, title, or interest in or to any Confidential Information, intellectual property, or other rights of any Covered Entity, whether by implication, estoppel, or otherwise. All Confidential Information remains the sole and exclusive property of the applicable Covered Entity or the Disclosing Party.

## 7. Return or Destruction

Upon written request by the Disclosing Party at any time, the Recipient shall promptly (and in any event within ten (10) days) return to the Disclosing Party, or permanently destroy, all Confidential Information in the Recipient's possession or control, including all copies, notes, and derivatives, and shall certify such return or destruction in writing upon request. The Recipient's obligations under this Agreement survive any such return or destruction.

## 8. Term and Survival

This Agreement is effective as of the Effective Date and continues in full force and effect **in perpetuity**, without expiration, unless and until replaced or superseded by a subsequent written agreement signed by both the Disclosing Party and the Recipient that expressly references and supersedes this Agreement. The Recipient's obligations of confidentiality and non-use apply to all Confidential Information disclosed at any time, whether before, during, or after any specific engagement or relationship between the parties.

## 9. No Obligation; No Partnership

Nothing in this Agreement obligates any Covered Entity or the Disclosing Party to disclose any Confidential Information, enter into any business relationship with the Recipient, pay the Recipient, or grant the Recipient access to any repository, system, or resource. This Agreement does not create any partnership, joint venture, employment, agency, or fiduciary relationship between the parties.

## 10. Remedies

The Recipient acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to one or more Covered Entities for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party (and any affected Covered Entity as a third-party beneficiary under Section 11) is entitled to seek **injunctive and equitable relief** in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, without the need to post bond or prove actual damages. The prevailing party in any action to enforce this Agreement is entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

## 11. Third-Party Beneficiaries

Each Covered Entity (including Covered Entities formed or acquired after the Effective Date) is an intended third-party beneficiary of this Agreement and may enforce it directly against the Recipient with respect to its own Confidential Information.

The parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in Snohomish County, Washington for any action arising out of or relating to this Agreement, except that the Disclosing Party may seek injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction.

No amendment or waiver of any provision is effective unless in writing and signed by both parties (a merged pull request to this repository modifying this file, with David Cahill's approval and the Recipient's signed commit, satisfies this requirement).

## 15. Assignment
